<div class="grid">

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Executive Summary</h2>
    <p>
      Maximo becomes a strategic platform only when it is anchored to business priorities, executed with discipline,
      and governed with trusted data. This scorecard provides a fast, structured way for leaders to evaluate readiness
      across the dimensions that determine executive value: alignment, reliability execution, and governance.
    </p>

    <p style="margin-top:14px;">
      Use it to establish a shared baseline, identify the highest-impact gaps, and focus modernization and improvement
      investments where they will materially improve risk, cost, uptime, and compliance outcomes.
    </p>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>Who This Is For</h2>
    <ul>
      <li><strong>Asset &amp; Reliability Leaders</strong> aligning work execution to measurable performance outcomes</li>
      <li><strong>Operations &amp; Maintenance Executives</strong> prioritizing investments to reduce unplanned work and recurring failures</li>
      <li><strong>IT &amp; EAM Product Owners</strong> clarifying platform priorities, integration scope, and governance ownership</li>
      <li><strong>Finance &amp; Risk Stakeholders</strong> seeking clearer linkage between Maximo, risk exposure, and accountability</li>
    </ul>

    <h2 style="margin-top:18px;">How to Use It</h2>
    <ol>
      <li>Score each item from <strong>0–2</strong> based on what is consistently true today.</li>
      <li>Total your score and review the maturity interpretation.</li>
      <li>Prioritize the top gaps that most constrain executive outcomes (risk, cost, uptime, compliance).</li>
      <li>Translate gaps into a focused improvement plan with accountable owners and time-bound milestones.</li>
    </ol>
  </section>

  <section class="card">
    <h2>What It Measures</h2>
    <p>
      The scorecard evaluates the readiness signals leaders care about—whether decisions are aligned, execution is predictable,
      and governance supports scale.
    </p>

    <ul>
      <li><strong>Strategy &amp; Business Alignment</strong> — shared definitions, priorities tied to business outcomes, KPI accountability</li>
      <li><strong>Work &amp; Reliability Execution</strong> — planned vs. unplanned visibility, standardized job plans/PMs, failure coding discipline</li>
      <li><strong>Data, Integration &amp; Governance</strong> — inventory strategy support, ERP/ops integration, business-led governance ownership</li>
    </ul>

    <p style="margin-top:14px;">
      The result is a clear, executive-friendly view of whether Maximo is positioned to drive performance—or will remain transactional.
    </p>
  </section>
