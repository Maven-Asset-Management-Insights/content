<div class="glossary">

<h2 id="A">A</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Asset Criticality</h3>
<p>A method for ranking assets based on risk (safety, environment, production, cost). Criticality helps prioritize reliability work, PM optimization, spares strategy, and where Maximo data standards must be strongest.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Asset Health Index (AHI)</h3>
<p>A consolidated metric representing the overall condition of an asset, typically combining multiple data points (such as condition measurements, failure history, and performance trends) into a single health score.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Asset Hierarchy</h3>
<p>The structured way assets are organized in your EAM system (site → system → equipment → component). A good hierarchy reflects how the organization operates and maintains assets. A weak hierarchy makes reporting misleading and reliability analysis unreliable.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Asset Lifecycle Cost</h3>
<p>The total cost of owning and operating an asset over its entire lifecycle, from acquisition through operation, maintenance, and disposal. This includes acquisition cost, maintenance expenses, operating costs, and end-of-life removal or replacement costs.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Asset Performance</h3>
<p>How well assets deliver intended outcomes over time (availability, reliability, cost, risk), supported by good data and disciplined execution.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Asset Performance Management (APM)</h3>
<p>A capability set that connects asset condition, risk, and performance data to decisions—helping organizations optimize maintenance, reduce downtime, and manage lifecycle cost. APM depends on strong foundations in hierarchy, work management, and data standards.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="B">B</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Backlog Health</h3>
<p>A view of whether the maintenance backlog is manageable and prioritized. Healthy backlogs are risk-based and achievable; unhealthy backlogs grow without clear priorities and hide reliability risk.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Maintenance Backlog Ratio</h3>
<p>A performance measure that compares the amount of overdue or outstanding maintenance work to total maintenance workload, indicating scheduling pressure and potential reliability risk.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="C">C</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Condition-Based Maintenance (CBM)</h3>
<p>A maintenance strategy where actions are taken based on actual asset condition indicators (such as vibration, temperature, or oil quality) rather than on a fixed schedule. Condition-based maintenance aims to perform work only when needed, improving efficiency and reducing unplanned failures.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Condition Monitoring</h3>
<p>Tracking asset condition indicators (vibration, temperature, oil analysis, run hours, alarms) to detect emerging problems before failure.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="D">D</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Data Governance</h3>
<p>The standards, ownership, and routines that keep Maximo/EAM data accurate and consistent over time.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Data Lineage</h3>
<p>Documentation showing where data originated, how it has been transformed, and where it is used across systems. Data lineage supports trustworthy reporting, analytics, and governance in asset performance platforms.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Digital Twin</h3>
<p>A digital replica of a physical asset or system that integrates real-time sensor and operational data with analytical models. Digital twins are used to monitor performance, test scenarios, and support predictive maintenance and performance optimization.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="E">E</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
  <h3>Enterprise Asset Management (EAM)</h3>
  <p>
    A structured approach to managing physical assets from acquisition through retirement. EAM integrates work management, asset data, maintenance strategy, and lifecycle cost control to improve reliability, reduce risk, and support long-term operational performance.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Enterprise Asset Management (EAM) Strategy</h3>
<p>A high-level plan that aligns asset management practices, investment decisions, and lifecycle priorities with business objectives to maximize asset value and minimize risk.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="F">F</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Failure Code</h3>
<p>A standardized way to record what failed and why. Consistent failure codes turn work history into reliability insight.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Failure Mode</h3>
<p>The specific way an asset fails (e.g., bearing seizure, seal leak, motor winding failure).</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Failure Modes, Effects, and Criticality Analysis (FMECA)</h3>
<p>A structured method for identifying potential asset failure modes, evaluating their effects, and ranking them by severity and likelihood so that risk-based mitigation and maintenance strategies can be developed.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="I">I</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Inventory Optimization</h3>
<p>The process of balancing spare parts availability with carrying and procurement costs to ensure that necessary parts are on hand when needed without excess inventory.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="K">K</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Key Risk Indicator (KRI)</h3>
<p>A metric that signals increasing exposure to risk in asset performance, reliability, or operations, enabling proactive planning and corrective action.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>KPI (Key Performance Indicator)</h3>
<p>A metric used to track progress toward operational outcomes. Effective KPIs are tied to decisions and behaviors.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="M">M</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Master Data Management (MDM)</h3>
<p>A governance discipline that ensures key asset data (such as identifiers, classifications, and specifications) is consistent, accurate, and shared across systems and teams.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)</h3>
<p>A reliability metric representing the average time between failures for a repairable asset.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Mean Time To Repair (MTTR)</h3>
<p>The average time required to restore an asset after failure.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="O">O</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Operational Availability (Ao)</h3>
<p>The probability that an asset is ready for use and available when required, taking into account maintenance downtime and reliability performance.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="P">P</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Planned vs. Unplanned Work</h3>
<p>Planned work is prepared in advance. Unplanned work is reactive.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Planning and Scheduling</h3>
<p>Planning defines the job. Scheduling assigns timing and sequence.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>PM Compliance</h3>
<p>A measure of whether preventive maintenance work is completed as planned.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Predictive Analytics</h3>
<p>Data-driven techniques that analyze historical and real-time information to anticipate future asset behavior or failures, helping organizations forecast maintenance needs and optimize asset decisions.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Predictive Maintenance (PdM)</h3>
<p>Maintenance triggered by measured condition or predicted failure risk.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Prescriptive Maintenance</h3>
<p>An advanced maintenance approach that combines predictive insights with specific recommended actions designed to optimize asset reliability, reduce risk, and control costs.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Remaining Useful Life (RUL)</h3>
<p>An estimate of the operating time remaining before an asset or component is expected to reach the end of its useful service life, often used in predictive maintenance planning.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Work Prioritization Index (WPI)</h3>
<p>A scoring method used to rank maintenance tasks based on risk, impact, criticality, and resource requirements, helping teams focus efforts where they add the most value.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="R">R</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Reliability Strategy</h3>
<p>The set of decisions that define what maintenance is performed, how often, and why—based on risk and failure modes.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Root Cause Analysis (RCA)</h3>
<p>A systematic problem-solving approach used to identify the fundamental causes of failures or performance issues, with the goal of preventing recurrence rather than addressing symptoms.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="S">S</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Service Level Agreement (SLA)</h3>
<p>An agreement that defines expected levels of service or performance, often between maintenance teams and internal stakeholders, to establish clear expectations for asset support and responsiveness.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Standard Job Plan</h3>
<p>A reusable work template that defines how a job should be performed.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Standardized Failure Codes</h3>
<p>A controlled set of failure descriptors used to classify failure events consistently across an organization, enabling reliable analysis and benchmarking of work history.</p>
</div>

<h2 id="W">W</h2>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Work Management Discipline</h3>
<p>The habits and standards that make work predictable.</p>
</div>

<div class="glossary-term">
<h3>Work Order Closeout Quality</h3>
<p>How consistently teams capture the information needed at completion.</p>
</div>

</div>
