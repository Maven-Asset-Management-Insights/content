If Maximo isn't delivering what you expected, the fix rarely starts with the software. It starts with four fundamentals most programs skip or underinvest in.

## Start here — in this order.

**Validate the [asset hierarchy](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-hierarchy).** Is it accurate? Does it reflect how the plant or facility is actually structured? If assets are classified inconsistently across sites, maintenance history is fragmented and reporting is misleading before anyone runs a single query. Weak [hierarchy integrity](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#hierarchy-integrity) undermines every performance metric built on top of it.

**Standardize [work order closeout](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#work-order-closeout).** Define what's required at closeout — [failure codes](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#failure-code), labor, downtime, completion notes — and make it non-negotiable. This is where [closeout quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#closeout-quality) is either created or lost, and it has a direct impact on [failure data quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#failure-data-quality) downstream.

**Establish [governance](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-governance).** Monthly [data quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-quality) reviews. Quarterly [PM strategy](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#preventive-maintenance-pm) refreshes. Clear ownership of standards through [data standard enforcement](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-standard-enforcement). Governance is what keeps Maximo valuable after the implementation team is gone — without it, [data drift](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-drift) sets in and the program quietly loses ground.

**Build reporting that drives decisions, not dashboards.** A dashboard that no one acts on isn't reporting. Know what decisions need to be made, and build the [KPIs](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#key-performance-indicator-kpi) and reports that inform them. [Data confidence](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-confidence) is what separates a report leadership acts on from one they quietly ignore.

None of this is complicated. All of it requires [work management discipline](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#work-management-discipline). That's the work.
