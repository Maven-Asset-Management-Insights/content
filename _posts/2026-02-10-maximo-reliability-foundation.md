[Advanced analytics](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-performance-management-apm), [predictive maintenance](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#predictive-maintenance-pdm), MAS AI capabilities — all of it depends on a foundation most organizations haven't fully built yet.

## Three fundamentals that have to come first

**1. An [asset hierarchy](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-hierarchy) that matches reality.** If assets aren't structured consistently, [hierarchy integrity](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#hierarchy-integrity) breaks down, maintenance history is fragmented, and reporting is meaningless.

**2. [Work management discipline](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#work-management-discipline).** Consistent planning, [failure coding](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#failure-code), and [closeout quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#closeout-quality) are what turn [work orders](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#work-order-wo) into [usable data](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#usable-data). Without them, the history in Maximo reflects workarounds, not actual work.

**3. [Governance](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-governance) that doesn't fade.** Someone has to be accountable for keeping standards alive after the project team leaves. Monthly [data quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-quality) reviews, quarterly [PM](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#preventive-maintenance-pm) updates, defined ownership through [data standard enforcement](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-standard-enforcement) — that's what separates improvement that lasts from improvement that stalls.

Get these right first. Everything advanced becomes significantly easier — and significantly more valuable — when they're in place.
