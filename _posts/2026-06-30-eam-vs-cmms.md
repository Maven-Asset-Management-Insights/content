If you ask a room full of asset management pros to explain the difference between a [CMMS](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#computerized-maintenance-management-system-cmms) and an [EAM](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#enterprise-asset-management-eam), you'll probably get a lot of blank stares and a few nervous laughs. This isn't just a matter of definitions. When teams mix up these systems, it leads to wasted potential, mismatched goals, and missed ROI—especially for anyone running IBM Maximo or MAS.

Here's the real difference: it's all about scope.

A CMMS is all about the tasks. It's built to answer: What maintenance needs doing, and did we get it done? That's perfect if you've got one site, a tight asset list, and a team that's laser-focused on keeping things running day in and day out.

[EAM](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#enterprise-asset-management-eam) takes things to the next level. It manages your physical assets from the moment you buy them to the day you retire them. That means tying in finance, procurement, compliance, [reliability strategy](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#reliability-strategy), workforce management, and now, all the cool stuff like AI analytics and IoT sensors. IBM Maximo Application Suite is a true enterprise EAM platform, built for organizations that run big, complex operations.

But here's the kicker: a lot of organizations are using their EAM like it's just a CMMS.

How can you tell? The system is just a work order machine. Finance, procurement, and ops are left out of the loop. [Asset records](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset) show maintenance history but skip the costs and lifecycle forecasts. Purchasing is still happening in spreadsheets. Compliance docs are tracked by hand. Leadership is making big budget calls without a single data point from the asset management system.

Sound familiar? The problem isn't your software. IBM Maximo and MAS can do it all. The real question is: are you using your EAM for everything it's built to handle?
