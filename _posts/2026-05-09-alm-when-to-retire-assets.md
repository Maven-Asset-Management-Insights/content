Every [asset](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-maximo) has its day. Eventually, the cost to keep it running outpaces the value it brings. That's your retirement threshold — and it's one of the biggest calls you'll make in asset lifecycle management.

Most teams miss the mark here — not because they don't know what they're doing, but because they're flying blind without the right data.

## To make the right call, you need the full story.

[Asset lifecycle cost](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-lifecycle-cost), downtime history, current condition tracked through the [asset health index](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-health-index-ahi), [remaining useful life](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#remaining-useful-life-rul), and what it'll cost to replace. If you've got all that, the answer is usually clear. But if your maintenance logs are spotty, [failure codes](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#failure-code) are missing, or [asset records](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset-maximo) are a mess — the result of poor [data quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-quality) compounding over time — you're left guessing, basing big decisions on age, gut instinct, or whatever the budget cycle says.

Let's be honest: none of those are signals you can trust. That's a [data confidence](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-confidence) problem, not a judgment problem.

This is where good lifecycle management really shines. If your [EAM](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#enterprise-asset-management-eam) platform has tracked every step from procurement to daily operation — [work order closeout](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#work-order-closeout), [failure data quality](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#failure-data-quality), [condition monitoring](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#condition-monitoring) — you've got everything you need to make a solid, data-backed retirement decision. But that only works if you've been capturing the right data all along.

Retirement is the final chapter for your [asset](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#asset) — but how well you write that ending depends on every page that came before. The foundation is [data discipline](https://maven-asset-management-insights.github.io/content/glossary/#data-discipline), built and maintained through every stage of the lifecycle.
